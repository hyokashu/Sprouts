# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping segments: serious collectors (hunt CN-exclusive graded/rare items for prestige and investment; follow the hobby closely; compare grading companies; care deeply about authenticity provenance) and casual fans (Pokémon and One Piece enthusiasts who want sealed product to open; driven by game affinity rather than collector value). Both are Australian. Both are unlikely to find CN-exclusive product elsewhere in the country.

## Product Purpose

Vault TCG is the dedicated Australian access point for Chinese-market-exclusive TCG products: Pokémon CN and One Piece CN sealed sets, displays, and graded cards. Success means being the first name Australian collectors and fans think of when CN product drops — trusted for authenticity, fast Melbourne dispatch, and real collector knowledge.

## Brand Personality

Rare. Expert. Collector-grade. Voice is confident and knowledgeable without being hype-driven. Collectors don't want to be sold to — they want to be informed. Tagline: "Rarity is a virtue."

## Anti-references

Generic Shopify store: bland white/grey palette, Dawn-theme aesthetic, product grid with zero brand identity, copy that could be any online retailer. Vault TCG should feel unmistakably itself — not like a template someone deployed last month.

## Design Principles

1. **Rarity speaks for itself** — Let the product image carry the page. Restraint around the product, not noise.
2. **Expert voice** — Every copy block assumes the reader knows their hobby. No hand-holding, no buzzwords.
3. **Zone integrity** — Each TCG universe (Pokémon, One Piece) has its own visual energy. Honour it; don't flatten everything into a single brand colour.
4. **Provenance is the product** — Authenticity, direct import, and Melbourne dispatch are core differentiators. Surface them at every logical touchpoint, not just in an about page.
5. **Speed over ceremony** — Collectors on the hunt want to find and buy fast. Navigation and product discovery must be frictionless.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG compliance target. Flag obvious blockers (keyboard traps, missing labels, broken focus management). WCAG AA contrast as a practical floor.
